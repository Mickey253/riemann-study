Riemann Spaces Study

Hello Participants,
Thanks for joining the "Riemann Spaces" study! We're looking into how visualization of graphs in different ways can affect how people interact with graphs in different ways. Graphs, for this study, are considered as networks that show connections between things. Our study is all about exploring how people understand these maps in regular 2D (like paper) and other not-so-regular spaces like on a ball or a strange-shaped surface.

Introduction to Test

In this test, we want to see how well you get along with different kinds of graphs. We'll show you some in the usual flat way and then in some different, kind of strange ways. Based on different visualizations, you will be answering some questions which can invlove counting, calculation and estimation. You can use different interactions to make your task easier and the interactions you can use are listed below:
  • Zoom and Pan: You can scroll with a mouse to zoom in and out and drag the graph for panning.
  • Hover: By hovering over nodes, you will be able to see the hovered node highlighted (red) and the neighbors highlighted (yellow) along with them.
  • Double click: Double clicking anywhere on canvas (white space where the graph is located) will recenter the point you clicked to the center of your screen.

Consent

Before we get started, here are a few important things to know:
  • Informed Consent: You're here because you want to be! If you have questions, just ask before saying you're okay to start.
